# Resume Polish Prompt Template

Use this template when reading the user's resume and preparing a tailored pitch for each employer.

---

## Your job here

You're not rewriting the resume. You're extracting the *right slice* of it -- the 2-3 experiences and
skills that will land hardest with *this specific employer*.

Every employer is different. A hospital dietary department cares about consistency, food safety, and
reliability. A wood-fired pizza place cares about quality, creativity, and working clean. A 24-hour
diner cares about speed, volume, and keeping your head when it's slammed. Your job is to read both the
resume AND the employer profile, and find where they intersect.

---

## Step 1: Read the resume as a whole

Before doing anything per-employer, understand the full person:

- What's their most senior role? What were they actually responsible for?
- What environments have they worked in? (Fast food, fine dining, corporate chain, mom-and-pop?)
- What's their strongest skill cluster? (Speed/volume? Leadership? Precision? Versatility?)
- Are there any achievements worth calling out? (Ran overnight solo, trained new staff, managed inventory?)
- What's their "voice" -- are they formal or casual in how they describe their work?

Write a one-paragraph summary of the person as a worker. Keep this in mind throughout.

---

## Step 2: For each employer, produce a "pitch profile"

**Format:**

```
EMPLOYER: [Business Name]
TYPE: [e.g., casual diner / upscale wine bar / hospital dietary / sports bar]
TONE: [formal / semi-formal / casual]

MOST RELEVANT EXPERIENCES:
1. [Job Title at Employer] -- [1-2 sentences on why this experience speaks to THIS employer]
2. [Job Title at Employer] -- [1-2 sentences]
3. (optional 3rd if genuinely relevant)

KEY SKILLS TO HIGHLIGHT:
- [Skill] -- [why it matters to this employer specifically]
- [Skill] -- [why it matters]

WHAT TO DOWNPLAY / NOT MENTION:
- [Any experiences that are irrelevant or might create wrong impressions]

HOOK: [One sentence that opens the email memorably -- leads with the strongest connection]
```

---

## Step 3: Gut-check

Before moving to email drafting, ask yourself:

- Does the pitch feel like it came from *this person*, or does it sound generic?
- Would a hiring manager at *this specific place* care about what you're highlighting?
- Is there anything in the resume you're NOT using that might actually be the most compelling thing for this employer?

If the answer to that last one is yes -- use it.

---

## Examples

**Resume line:** "Graveyard cook and shift manager at Waffle House"

- For Harbor Grill (high-volume diner): "I ran overnight shifts at Waffle House solo -- that's
  high-volume, relentless ticket flow, and you're accountable for everything. I'm built for exactly
  the pace a place like Harbor Grill runs."

- For Elm Street Bistro (wine bar, small plates): "My Waffle House time taught me speed and
  consistency under pressure. What I'm excited to bring here is that precision -- but applied to a
  menu I'm genuinely proud of."

- For Maplewood Medical Center (dietary): "I've worked overnight solo service, which means food safety,
  cleanliness, and reliability aren't things I learned from a manual -- they're just how I operate."

Same resume line. Three completely different angles. That's the work.
