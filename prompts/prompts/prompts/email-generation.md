# Email Generation Prompt Template

Use this template when drafting tailored application emails for each employer.

---

## Your job here

Write an email that a real person would actually send. Not a cover letter. Not a form letter.
An email -- the kind you'd fire off if a friend said "hey, [place] is hiring, you should reach out."

The people reading these are usually busy. They're a chef mid-prep, a manager between shifts, or an
HR person sorting through a stack. Your email has maybe 10 seconds before they decide whether to keep
reading. Make those 10 seconds count.

---

## Principles

**Short.** Under 200 words for the body. Under 150 is better.

**Specific.** Don't say "I'm passionate about food service." Say something concrete about what you've
actually done and what that means for this employer. Specificity = credibility.

**Matched to the place.** A hospital dietary email should be professional and reliability-focused.
A casual neighborhood bar email can have warmth and personality. Read the room.

**Sounds like the person.** Read the resume for voice cues. Match that energy.

**One clear ask.** End with: come in, call me, let's talk.

---

## Email structure

Opening (1-2 sentences): Who you are and why you're writing. Lead with the strongest connection to
*this* employer. Skip "My name is X and I am writing to apply" -- that's filler.

Middle (2-4 sentences): Your 2-3 most relevant experiences/skills, framed for this employer.
Be specific. Use real job titles, real situations, real skills.

Closing (1-2 sentences): Your ask. Keep it warm and direct.

Signature: Full Name, Phone, Email

---

## Subject lines

Keep it simple and clear:

- Cook Application -- [Name], Available Now
- Experienced Line Cook Seeking Position -- [Name]
- Kitchen Staff Application -- [Name]

Avoid vague ("Job Inquiry"), overly formal ("Application for the Position of Cook"), or gimmicky lines.

---

## Examples

**Casual neighborhood diner:**

Subject: Cook Application -- Jordan Rivera, Available Now

Hi there,

I'm reaching out about cook positions at Morning Glory Diner. I've been working in fast-paced
breakfast and diner kitchens for four years -- most recently as a line cook where I handled grill
and egg station during our busiest weekend rushes.

I'm solid on the full line, I work clean, and I don't fall apart when it gets slammed. I'm local
and available to start quickly.

Resume attached. I'd love to come meet the team whenever works for you.

Jordan Rivera
555-867-5309
jordan.rivera@email.com

**Hospital / institutional:**

Subject: Cook Position Application -- Jordan Rivera

Dear Hiring Team,

I am writing to apply for a cook or dietary staff position. My background includes kitchen
management, high-volume food service, and consistent adherence to food safety standards.

I understand that in a healthcare setting, consistency and reliability are not optional -- and
those are the qualities I'm most proud of. I've run overnight shifts solo, managed prep teams,
and kept kitchens clean and organized under pressure.

Resume attached. I would welcome the opportunity to discuss any current openings.

Sincerely,
Jordan Rivera
555-867-5309
jordan.rivera@email.com

---

## Final checks before outputting each email

- Under 200 words in the body?
- Does it mention something specific to *this* employer?
- Does the tone match the employer type?
- Is the subject line clear and direct?
- Does it end with a real ask?
- Is the signature complete (name, phone, email)?
- Remind the user to attach their resume before sending.
